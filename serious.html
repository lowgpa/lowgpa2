Reality Check

This path is not for everyone.

Studying in Germany is not a "bought" degree. It is hard to get in, and harder to graduate. If you have a low GPA, you have already used up your margin for error.

1. No Hand-Holding

German universities expect you to be an adult. There is nobody to remind you of deadlines. Nobody to tell you which courses to pick. If you miss a deadline by 1 minute, you are out.

2. The "Easy" Unis are Traps

Private universities often market heavily to low GPA students. They cost €10k-€15k per year. While the degree is valid, the reputation is not the same as public universities, and employers know the difference. We strongly advise aiming for public universities if possible.

3. Language is Survival

You might study in English, but your life is in German. rental contracts, government bureaucracy, buying groceries. Without B1 German, you will struggle socially and mentally.

The Bottom Line

If you are willing to learn German to B2 level, write your own motivation letters, and research every single module of your target course, you can make it regardless of your GPA. If you want an agent to do it for you, you will likely fail or get scammed.

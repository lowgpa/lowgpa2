The Protocol

This is a Strategic Operation. Not a Vacation.

Studying in Germany is not a "bought" degree. It is hard to get in, and harder to graduate. If you have a low GPA, you have already used up your margin for error. You must now play the game perfectly.

1. The "Ranking" Trap

The Lie: "I need to go to TU Munich or RWTH Aachen to get a good job."

The Truth: Rankings in Germany are for research output, not employer preference. A degree from a small "Fachhochschule" (FH) in a village is legally and professionally equal to a degree from a top TU.

Strategy: Employers care about skills and Werkstudent (part-time work) experience. A 2.5 GPA at a famous uni is worse than a 2.5 GPA at a smaller uni if the smaller uni gave you time to work and gain experience.

2. University vs. FH (Fachhochschule)

Most low GPA applicants ignore FHs (Universities of Applied Sciences). This is a fatal mistake.
